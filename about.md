## What is ShieldSmart?

ShieldSmart is an independent, educational website dedicated to one goal: making digital security accessible to ordinary people.

We don't assume you're a developer, a sysadmin, or particularly "techy." We assume you're a person who uses the internet, stores things there, and would like those things to remain private and safe.

All of our content is written in plain English. No jargon without explanation. No assumption that you already know what a "threat actor" is or why "zero-day exploits" matter. We start from the beginning.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### "I'm just a normal person. Do I really need to worry about this?"

Yes—and here's why the "I'm not interesting enough" logic breaks down.

Modern cyberattacks are largely automated. Criminals don't sit at computers and decide to hack *you* specifically. They run software that tests millions of accounts simultaneously, looking for any that have weak or reused passwords. If one of your accounts is on a list of leaked credentials (there's a good chance it is—billions of credentials are in circulation), their tools will find it.

You don't have to be famous, wealthy, or a political target to be affected by:

- **Credential stuffing** — your leaked password tried across other sites
- **Ransomware** — malware that encrypts your files and demands payment
- **Phishing** — fake emails designed to steal your login or payment information
- **Identity fraud** — your personal details used to open accounts in your name

### "I've never been hacked. Why change what's working?"

Most people don't know they've been hacked until long after it happens—if ever. Attackers often stay quiet in compromised accounts for weeks or months, harvesting information or waiting for the right moment. The absence of an obvious incident isn't evidence of safety.

Additionally, the threat landscape evolves constantly. Security habits that were adequate five years ago may not be today. Checking in periodically is worthwhile.

### "Isn't security software just for businesses?"

No. Many of the best tools—Bitwarden, ProtonVPN (free tier), uBlock Origin, Malwarebytes—are free and designed specifically for individual users. The basic security stack we recommend costs nothing and takes an afternoon to set up.

### "Won't using all these tools make my life complicated?"

Less than you'd think. The upfront investment—installing a password manager, enabling two-factor authentication—takes a couple of hours. After that, it largely runs in the background. Most users say a password manager actually makes life *easier* because they're no longer trying to remember or reset forgotten passwords.

### "How do you choose which tools to recommend?"

We evaluate tools on:

1. **Security** — Is the tool genuinely secure? Has it been audited?
2. **Privacy** — What data does it collect? Who can access it?
3. **Usability** — Can a non-technical person use it comfortably?
4. **Value** — Is the cost (if any) proportionate to the benefit?

We do not accept payment from tool vendors, and we do not use affiliate links. If that ever changes, we'll disclose it prominently.

## Our Philosophy

Security education too often falls into one of two traps:

**Fearmongering** — treating every online action as catastrophically risky, producing anxiety that leads to paralysis rather than action.

**Dismissiveness** — treating security as something only experts or organisations need to worry about, letting ordinary users feel they're exempt.

The reality is more useful: most serious threats are preventable with straightforward habits that anyone can build. The goal of ShieldSmart is to give you those habits—clearly, honestly, and without drama.
